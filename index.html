About

Guokun Dai is an Associate Professor at Fudan University. His research focuses on subseasonal to seasonal predictability, Arctic influences on Eurasian extreme weather and climate, and AI-enabled methods for atmospheric science.

He received his B.S. in Atmospheric Sciences from Nanjing University of Information Science and Technology in 2012, and his Ph.D. in Meteorology from the Institute of Atmospheric Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences in 2017.

He worked as a Postdoctoral Researcher and then Associate Research Scientist at Fudan University, served as a Visiting Scientist at the European Centre for Medium-Range Weather Forecasts (ECMWF), and has been Associate Professor at Fudan University since 2024.

Research Interests

Predictability

Predictability of mid-latitude weather and climate, with emphasis on extended-range and subseasonal forecasts.

Arctic–Midlatitude Linkages

Arctic sea-ice–air interactions and their impacts on Eurasian extreme cold events, Ural blocking, and NAO variability.

AI in Atmospheric Science

Applications of artificial intelligence and hybrid modeling to weather prediction, Earth system models, and ensemble forecasting.
